Aftenposten Design Challenge: Going the GEN Z Way in 2020 and Beyond

Project Context: Aftenposten goal is to reach out to young people between 18 to 25 years and to carve out ways to connect itself as a news brand to them.

Design Question How can Aftenposten present news and relevant context to young people (18 to 25 years) to increase use and engagement among them?

I love to start things by empathising with my users. For this, I believe a well-conducted research on the users and their in-depth analysis is the key. If done well, you cover up at least 60% of your path while designing a product for your users.

During our workings we first sorted out this group of 18 to 25 years old as ‘Gen Z’. Studying their characteristics, behaviour, news consumption habits became spontaneous.

Gen Z analysis and their Characteristics

Also known as ‘iGeneration’- count Greta Thunberg and Zendaya among others- are an entirely new cohort of consumers. Who are they? A generation born between 1996 to 2010. Young and vibrant. Most-ethnically diverse. Grew up with technology and social media. Embrace entrepreneurialism and are eco-conscious.

Key Points of Gen Z after User-Interview:

  1. They don’t feel connected with Aftenposten today
  2. Unwilling to pay for news or information as they think news should be free
  3. Identify with VG's news as it provides information and entertainment
  4. Like to view stories that they can share on social media
  5. Like to share their opinions about the society and the world

User-Needs Uncovered after Research:

  1. They wants the news reach out to them rather they seeking it
  2. Free of charge, unbiased, relevant, self-expression
  3. Video, stories, podcasts, photos, or other formats with a visual context
  4. Light reads in the morning
  5. In-depth articles in the afternoon and weekends

Concept Description

The Gen Z of the revamped Aftenposten, MODUS, posts articles that he or she comes across through feature utrop. Engages with like-minded youth. Get his or her voice heard and awarded. While Aftenposten receives the news and approves those that it thinks best matches the interest of Gen Zers.

From where this concept was formed? As I said empathising is more important with the users to strike a chord. Here lies the key! The birth of citizen journalism is often attributed to South Korea where the first platform of amateur generated information, OhMyNews, was created. The principle was simple; anyone can take part in the process of creating information – as the notion of participatory journalism (another term for citizen journalism) implies.

Key Takeaways:

  1. In the improved version, I brought utrop concept more in the forefront
  2. Removed the slidebar as it was difficult from a developer’s point of view
  3. Created a thread of: illustrated stories, documentaries and editor’s pick
  4. Placed hamburger menu
  5. Changed the icons
  6. Made the interface more minimalist

Results: How client used the design?

It was well-recieved by the and ‘utrop’ or citizen journalism was more talked among them. They encouraged us to bring this more in the forefront. Message from Espen Egil Hansen, Aftenposten, Ex-editor-in-chief and CEO, chose the design and concept of utrop or citizen journalism from the prototype designed was well-received.
