Designing and Coding a Game Project: Real-Fake News

Concept Description: A game for a minimum of 2 to 4 players called ‘Real Fake Gaming News’ has three levels of round- easy, medium, and hard. He or she uses fake coins to determine if the news on each news card is real or fake. Loses coins on the incorrect answer and retains them if the answer is correct.

A player accesses the companion website where he sees news cards with: a headline, a short story or description, and the source.

Phases Followed:

  1. Empathise: Used research papers from Medietilsynet to know the user-personas
  2. Define and Ideate: Brainstorming ideas to define the objective of the game
  3. Prototype and Design: Sketches, lo-fi wireframes, and hi-fi prototype
  4. Testing: User-testing of the prototypes and iterations

User-Testing and Iterations

Key Points:

  1. Liked the current news quiz
  2. Got an understanding of what’s real and unreal news
  3. A learning experience
  4. Found fun element in coin system
  5. Users felt intrigued while they were waiting for the results
  6. Were more open-mind towards reading news in English language

Iterations:

  1. The back side of the card has tips to identify the fake news
  2. Removed the time-frame of answering within 60 seconds
  3. The discussion and debate triggers cohesiveness and social interaction
  4. Score system is simple- to gain as many coins to win
